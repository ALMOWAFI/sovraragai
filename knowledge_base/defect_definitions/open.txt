Defect: Open (OP)
Code: OP | Group: copper_deficiency_defect | Default action: reject

What it is:
A break or gap in a copper conductor where continuity is required. The trace or via is physically interrupted, breaking the electrical path.

What it looks like:
Visible gap or thinning in a copper trace. May appear as a complete break, or as a very thin remaining section that has no conductivity. Sometimes only detectable under magnification or via electrical test.

Why it happens:
- Under-etching leaving copper too thin
- Mechanical damage during handling or depaneling
- Drill registration error cutting through a trace
- Via with no copper plating (missed plating step)
- Corroded or oxidized trace creating high-resistance open

Electrical impact:
Complete loss of signal or power on the affected net. Downstream components receive no supply or signal. Can cause entire functional block to fail.

Severity: Critical
An open on any required conductor is always a reject. No acceptable level of open circuit exists.

Action thresholds:
- Confirmed open on functional trace → reject
- Open on via → reject, verify via cross-section if needed for supplier feedback
- Suspected open, not electrically confirmed → run continuity test, do not pass without confirmation

Rework:
Not reworkable by standard SMT rework. This is a fabrication defect.
Reject and return to PCB supplier. Document with image and electrical test data.
In some cases, jumper wire repair is permitted for prototype or low-volume boards only — requires engineering sign-off and is not allowed for production or Class 3 assemblies.
