DEFECT TYPE: Solder Bridge
CATEGORY: Soldering Defect
IPC CLASSIFICATION: IPC-A-610 Class 2/3 — Defect (Always Reject)

DEFINITION:
A solder bridge is an unintended solder connection between two or more conductors that should be electrically isolated. It creates an unwanted short circuit between adjacent pads, pins, traces, or components.

VISUAL CHARACTERISTICS:
- Visible solder filament or blob connecting adjacent pads or pins
- Often shiny metallic appearance bridging a gap between conductors
- May be very thin (hairline bridge) or thick (gross bridge)
- Common in fine-pitch components (0.4mm–0.8mm pitch QFP, BGA, 0201 passives)
- Detectable by AOI via contrast analysis of inter-pad gaps

TYPES OF SOLDER BRIDGES:
1. Pin-to-Pin Bridge: Between adjacent IC pins or component leads
2. Pad-to-Pad Bridge: Between adjacent SMD pads without components
3. Trace-to-Trace Bridge: Solder spanning between nearby PCB traces
4. Component-to-Ground Bridge: Lead shorting to ground plane or copper pour
5. BGA Column Bridge: Solder sphere merging between adjacent BGA balls

ROOT CAUSES:
- Excess solder paste volume (incorrect stencil aperture or printing pressure)
- Stencil misalignment causing paste displacement
- Component placement offset pushing paste outward
- Insufficient solder mask dam between pads (solder mask design error)
- Reflow profile issues (too slow ramp, insufficient peak temperature)
- Flux outgassing causing solder splatter
- Contaminated PCB surface reducing solder surface tension
- Manual soldering technique errors (excess solder, improper iron tip)
- Conveyor wave soldering with incorrect parameters

ELECTRICAL IMPACT:
- Direct short circuit between bridged conductors
- Overcurrent and potential component failure
- Logic errors in digital circuits (data bus corruption)
- Power supply damage if VCC bridged to GND
- Signal cross-talk or data corruption
- ESD vulnerability in bridged protection circuits

DETECTION METHODS:
- AOI: Primary detection — inter-pad gap analysis, coverage threshold algorithms
- X-ray inspection: Critical for BGA and hidden joints under packages
- Flying probe or ICT: Continuity testing catches electrical shorts
- Visual inspection under 10x–40x magnification
- Boundary scan (JTAG) for populated assemblies

SEVERITY CLASSIFICATION:
- ALL solder bridges are rejectable defects per IPC-A-610
- No bridge is acceptable regardless of board class or application
- Severity rating for prioritization:
  - HIGH: Bridge between power and signal nets
  - CRITICAL: Bridge between VCC and GND rails (potential immediate damage)
  - HIGH: Bridge between differential pair signals (immediate signal degradation)
  - MEDIUM: Bridge between isolated signal nets (functional failure)

ACCEPT/REJECT CRITERIA:
- REJECT: Any confirmed solder bridge between electrically isolated conductors
- No exceptions per IPC-A-610 Rev G, Section 7.3
- REWORK ELIGIBLE: If bridge can be removed without lifting pads or damaging component
- REJECT AFTER REWORK ATTEMPT: If pad lifting, component damage, or trace damage occurs during rework

REWORK PROCESS REFERENCE:
See SOP-RWK-002: Solder Bridge Removal Procedure
- Use solder wick with appropriate flux for bridge removal
- Verify with AOI re-inspection post-rework
- Maximum 2 rework attempts per location; reject after second failure
- BGA bridges require reballing; field rework not permitted for BGA shorts
