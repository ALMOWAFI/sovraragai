Defect: Mouse Bite (MB)
Code: MB | Group: copper_deficiency_defect | Default action: rework or reject

What it is:
Irregular edge erosion of a copper trace or pad, resembling small bites taken out of the conductor edge. Reduces the effective cross-section of the conductor.

What it looks like:
Scalloped or eroded edges along a copper trace or pad. The conductor is still mostly present but the edges are irregular and narrowed. Different from an open — the trace is not fully broken, just eaten away at the edges.

Why it happens:
- Over-etching during PCB fabrication
- Chemical contamination of etchant
- Poor resist adhesion allowing etchant to creep under the resist edge
- Mechanical abrasion during handling

Electrical impact:
Depends on severity. Minor mouse bite may only slightly increase trace resistance. Severe mouse bite reduces the conductor below its current-carrying capacity, causing localized heating and eventual open circuit under load.

Severity: Medium to High
- Minor (conductor width reduced <25%) → rework candidate or accept with engineering review
- Moderate (conductor width reduced 25–50%) → rework or reject depending on current requirement of that trace
- Severe (conductor width reduced >50%) → reject

Action thresholds:
- Measure remaining conductor width against design minimum width
- If remaining width meets minimum spec → may pass with engineering approval
- If below minimum spec → reject or attempt copper repair (specialist only)
- On high-current traces → apply stricter threshold, reject if any measurable reduction

Rework:
Minor cases: copper repair paste or conductive epoxy applied by qualified technician, followed by re-inspection.
Severe cases: reject, return to supplier.
Always re-inspect conductivity and resistance after any repair attempt.
