Defect: Short (SH)
Code: SH | Group: copper_residue_defect | Default action: reject

What it is:
Unintended copper connection between two conductors that should be isolated. Extra copper material bridges a gap and creates a short circuit.

What it looks like:
Copper trace or blob visually connecting two pads or traces that should have a gap between them. Usually appears as a thin or thick copper bridge under AOI.

Why it happens:
- Etching process did not fully remove copper between conductors
- Over-plating during PCB fabrication
- Copper splash or debris from drilling
- Design rule violation — conductors too close for the fab process capability

Electrical impact:
Direct short between the bridged nets. In power nets this can cause immediate overcurrent. In signal nets it causes logic errors or signal corruption. Always a functional failure.

Severity: Critical
No acceptable condition. Any confirmed short between isolated conductors is a reject.

Action thresholds:
- Confirmed short → reject, no exceptions
- Suspected short, not confirmed → verify with continuity test before routing to reject
- Short in power net (VCC/GND) → reject immediately, quarantine lot

Rework:
Generally not reworkable at board level — this is a fabrication defect. Reject and return to PCB supplier with defect documentation.
If the short is minor copper residue between pads (not a trace-to-trace fab defect), micro-abrasion by a qualified technician may be attempted once — but success rate is low and re-inspection is mandatory.
