Defect: Conductor Scratch (CS)
Code: CS | Group: conductor_scratch_defect | Default action: rework or reject

What it is:
A physical scratch or gouge on the surface of a copper conductor that reduces its cross-section or exposes the underlying layer. Caused by mechanical contact with a sharp object.

What it looks like:
Linear mark across a trace, usually oriented in one direction (tool or handling direction). May remove copper partially or fully across the scratch line. Scratch through the conductor looks like a partial open. Scratch through solder mask only looks like exposed copper with no visible conductor damage.

Why it happens:
- Handling damage — sharp objects, probes, fixtures
- AOI or test fixture contact during previous process step
- Board sliding against sharp edge during transport or storage
- Depaneling tooling contact

Electrical impact:
Depends on depth and location:
- Scratch through solder mask only → cosmetic, assess copper condition underneath
- Scratch partially through copper → increases resistance at that point, heating risk under load
- Scratch fully through copper → open circuit

Severity: Medium to High
- Solder mask scratch only, copper intact → pass or cosmetic rework
- Copper scratch, <25% depth → measure and compare to minimum conductor thickness spec
- Copper scratch, >25% depth or full open → reject

Action thresholds:
- Verify copper continuity across scratch with resistance measurement
- If resistance is within acceptable range and conductor cross-section meets spec → engineering review
- If resistance elevated or cross-section below spec → reject or specialist repair
- Scratch near or across a fine-pitch trace → apply stricter threshold

Rework:
Solder mask scratch: conformal coat or solder mask repair pen over exposed copper if no conductor damage.
Conductor scratch with partial copper removal: specialist copper repair, resistance verification required after.
Full open from scratch: reject.
