Defect: Spur (SP)
Code: SP | Group: copper_residue_defect | Default action: rework or reject

What it is:
A thin protrusion of copper extending from the edge of a conductor into a clearance area. Not a full bridge between conductors, but a copper spike or whisker that reduces the gap to adjacent conductors.

What it looks like:
Pointed or jagged copper extension off the edge of a trace or pad. Extends into the spacing area between conductors. May be very fine and only visible under magnification.

Why it happens:
- Under-etching leaving copper residue at trace edges
- Poor photoresist edge definition
- Copper redeposition during etching
- Mechanical damage or copper burr from drilling/routing

Electrical impact:
By itself a spur does not create a short, but it reduces the effective clearance between conductors. Under voltage stress this can lead to dielectric breakdown or dendritic growth bridging to the adjacent conductor. In high-frequency designs it also affects impedance.

Severity: Medium to High
Depends on how much it reduces the clearance:
- Spur reduces clearance by <25% → rework candidate
- Spur reduces clearance by 25–50% → rework required before passing
- Spur reduces clearance >50% or touches adjacent conductor → treat as Short, reject

Action thresholds:
- Measure remaining gap after spur
- Compare against design minimum clearance spec
- If gap still meets minimum clearance → engineering review, may pass
- If gap below minimum clearance → rework or reject

Rework:
Micro-abrasion or controlled etching by qualified technician to remove spur.
Re-inspect after removal — verify clearance meets spec and no adjacent conductor damage.
Maximum one rework attempt. If spur cannot be fully removed without damaging adjacent conductor, reject.
