DEFECT TYPE: Surface Crack
CATEGORY: Structural Integrity Defect
IPC CLASSIFICATION: IPC-A-610 Class 2/3 Critical Defect

DEFINITION:
A surface crack is a fracture or fissure that appears on the surface of a PCB substrate, solder mask, component body, or copper trace. Cracks compromise the mechanical and electrical integrity of the board.

VISUAL CHARACTERISTICS:
- Fine lines or fractures visible under AOI or microscopy
- May appear as hairline cracks or wider fissures
- Can propagate from vias, mounting holes, or stressed regions
- Often associated with thermal stress, mechanical shock, or poor lamination
- Color contrast between crack and surrounding material (darker line on light substrate)

TYPES OF SURFACE CRACKS:
1. Substrate Crack: Fracture in the PCB base material (FR4 or other laminate)
2. Solder Mask Crack: Fissure in the green (or other color) protective coating
3. Trace Crack: Break or partial break in copper conductor
4. Via Crack: Fracture around plated through-holes or vias
5. Component Body Crack: Physical damage to SMD or through-hole component housing

ROOT CAUSES:
- Thermal cycling stress (repeated heating/cooling during assembly or field use)
- Mechanical shock or vibration during handling or transport
- Excessive board flex or warpage during assembly
- Improper depaneling (routing, v-scoring with excessive force)
- Material defects in laminate or pre-existing damage
- Over-torquing of mechanical fasteners
- Insufficient corner relief at mounting points

ELECTRICAL IMPACT:
- Open circuits if cracks propagate through conductor traces
- Intermittent failures under thermal or mechanical stress
- Increased resistance at cracked conductors
- Signal integrity degradation in high-frequency circuits
- Potential short circuit if crack causes conductive debris bridging

DETECTION METHODS:
- Automated Optical Inspection (AOI) at 15-30 µm resolution
- X-ray inspection for subsurface cracks in multilayer boards
- In-circuit testing (ICT) for electrical continuity
- Dye and pry test for cross-sectional crack analysis
- Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM) for micro-crack analysis
- Thermal imaging under power to detect hot spots from cracked traces

SEVERITY CLASSIFICATION:
- LOW: Cosmetic crack in solder mask only, no underlying conductor affected, crack length <1mm
- MEDIUM: Crack in substrate or solder mask extending toward conductors, length 1-3mm
- HIGH: Crack reaching or touching conductor, via crack with potential open circuit
- CRITICAL: Confirmed open circuit from crack, crack crossing multiple traces

ACCEPT/REJECT CRITERIA PER IPC-A-610:
- Accept: Solder mask cracks that do not expose copper, no propagation risk
- Reject: Any crack that creates or risks creating an open circuit
- Reject: Cracks through vias in Class 3 (high reliability) applications
- Rework: Cracks contained to solder mask with no copper exposure, if conformal coating can seal

FIELD IMPLICATIONS:
Surface cracks can remain dormant under static conditions but cause intermittent failures under vibration, thermal cycling, or mechanical stress in field deployment. Boards with cracks in safety-critical systems must be rejected regardless of apparent electrical functionality.
