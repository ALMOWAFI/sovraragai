Defect: Conductor Foreign Object (CFO)
Code: CFO | Group: foreign_object_defect | Default action: rework

What it is:
Foreign material (not copper, not solder mask) found on or near a copper conductor. The object is in contact with or overlapping a conductor, creating a risk of contamination, corrosion, or electrical interference.

What it looks like:
Debris, particle, fiber, or residue sitting on top of or adjacent to a copper trace. May be metallic (conductive risk) or non-metallic (contamination risk). Visible under AOI as an anomaly on or near the conductor.

Types:
- Metallic foreign object: conductive particle, solder ball, tin whisker, metal swarf — high risk
- Non-metallic foreign object: fiber, dust, resin residue, flux residue — lower risk but still contamination

Why it happens:
- Contamination during assembly — airborne particles landing on board
- Solder splash from nearby soldering operation
- Flux residue not cleaned
- Lint or fiber from handling (gloves, wipes)
- Swarf from nearby mechanical operations (drilling, routing)

Electrical impact:
Metallic object on a conductor can bridge to an adjacent conductor causing a short. Non-metallic contamination can cause corrosion under humid conditions or act as a moisture trap. Severity scales with conductivity of the foreign object and its proximity to adjacent conductors.

Severity: High (metallic) / Medium (non-metallic)
- Metallic particle on conductor or in gap between conductors → treat as potential short, high priority rework
- Non-metallic residue on conductor → rework before passing, document

Action thresholds:
- Any metallic foreign object → rework immediately, verify clearance after removal
- Non-metallic object on conductor → rework, clean, re-inspect
- Object fully between two conductors with no gap → reject if cannot be safely removed

Rework:
Remove with ESD-safe tweezers or brush under magnification.
Do not drag across conductors — lift off vertically.
Clean area with IPA after removal.
Re-inspect to verify conductor is undamaged and no residue remains.
