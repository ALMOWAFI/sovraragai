DEFECT TYPE: Tombstoning (Manhattan Effect / Drawbridging)
CATEGORY: Soldering / Placement Defect
IPC CLASSIFICATION: IPC-A-610 — Defect for Class 2/3

DEFINITION:
Tombstoning (also called the Manhattan effect or drawbridging) occurs when a two-terminal SMD component (resistor, capacitor, inductor) stands up on one end during reflow soldering, resembling a tombstone. One pad is soldered while the other end lifts off the board.

VISUAL CHARACTERISTICS:
- Component standing vertically or at an angle (>10° from horizontal)
- One solder joint formed normally; opposite end lifted from pad
- Clear visual indication: component not lying flat on board
- Empty or partially-filled pad visible at lifted end
- Easily detected by AOI side-view cameras or angled illumination

ROOT CAUSES:
The primary cause is unequal solder surface tension forces pulling on the two component terminations during reflow:
- Thermal imbalance: One pad reaches liquidus temperature before the other
  → Liquid solder surface tension pulls component upward on one side
- Paste volume imbalance: Unequal solder paste deposits on opposing pads
- Component misalignment during placement (offset from pad center)
- Pad design imbalance: Unequal pad sizes causing asymmetric heat absorption
- Large copper pours connected to one pad acting as heat sink
- Contaminated paste or incorrect reflow profile (too fast ramp rate)
- Component shift during board handling before reflow

COMPONENT TYPES MOST AFFECTED:
- 0402, 0201, 01005 passive components (most susceptible due to small size)
- Small SOD/SOT packages
- Two-terminal ferrite beads
- Small tantalum and ceramic capacitors
- Less common in 0805 and larger due to greater weight

ELECTRICAL IMPACT:
- Open circuit at lifted termination
- Partial or intermittent connection depending on lift angle
- Circuit malfunction proportional to component function
- No short circuit (opposite of solder bridge)
- Decoupling capacitor tombstoning → power supply noise vulnerability
- Filter network open → EMI compliance failure

SEVERITY CLASSIFICATION:
- HIGH to CRITICAL depending on component function
- All tombstoning is a defect per IPC-A-610
- No acceptable tombstoning angle for Class 2/3

ACCEPT/REJECT CRITERIA PER IPC-A-610 Rev G:
- REJECT: Any component with >0% lift from pad (Class 3 — High Reliability)
- REJECT: Component body angle >10° from board surface (Class 2)
- REWORK ELIGIBLE: Can be reflowed and repositioned if pads are undamaged
- INSPECT POST-REWORK: Verify no pad lifting or damage

PREVENTION MEASURES:
- Balanced pad design per IPC-7351 (equal pad dimensions for two-terminal passives)
- Thermal relief on large copper pours connected to pads
- Optimized solder paste volume (stencil aperture)
- Controlled reflow profile ramp rate (1.5–3°C/sec recommended)
- Component placement centering within 25% of pad overlap
- Nitrogen atmosphere reflow for fine-pitch components

REWORK PROCEDURE:
1. Apply flux to lifted pad
2. Use hot air rework station to reflow both joints simultaneously
3. Gently press component flat with tweezers while solder is liquid
4. Inspect and verify both solder joints meet IPC-A-610 criteria
5. Re-inspect by AOI
6. Maximum 1 rework attempt; reject if unsuccessful or if pad damage occurs
