QUALITY CRITERIA DOCUMENT
QCD-001: PCB Assembly PASS Thresholds
Revision: 4.1 | Effective Date: 2024-03-01 | Owner: Quality Engineering
Conformance Standard: IPC-A-610 Rev G

1. PURPOSE
This document defines the acceptance criteria for PCB assemblies to receive PASS disposition and proceed to next process step or shipment. All criteria must be met simultaneously for a PASS disposition.

2. SOLDER JOINT PASS CRITERIA

2.1 SMD Solder Joints (all classes unless noted):
    - Fillet appearance: Bright, smooth, concave meniscus
    - Wetting: ≥75% of pad area wetted for Class 2; ≥85% for Class 3
    - Solder height: Extends to component termination; does not exceed component body
    - No visible voids on joint surface (subsurface voids <25% of joint area per X-ray, Class 2)
    - No bridging to adjacent conductors

2.2 Chip Component (Resistors, Capacitors, Inductors) Pass Criteria:
    - Solder fillet present on both ends
    - Minimum end cap coverage: 25% of component end cap height
    - Maximum solder height: Does not exceed top of component body
    - Component alignment: Side overhang ≤25% of component width
    - Component flatness: ≤5° tilt from PCB surface
    - No cracks, chips, or damage to component body visible at 10x

2.3 IC Gull-Wing (QFP, SOP, SOIC) Pass Criteria:
    - Heel fillet: Present and visible on all leads
    - Toe fillet: Present (minimum 50% of lead width)
    - Side fillet: Present on at least one side of lead
    - Lead alignment: ≤25% overhang from pad
    - Pitch-to-solder relationship: Solder does not bridge between adjacent leads
    - Lead coplanarity: All leads within 0.1mm of pad surface (per IPC standard)

2.4 BGA Pass Criteria (X-ray required):
    - Ball collapse: 40%–60% of original ball diameter (normal reflow)
    - No missing balls (ball count matches package specification)
    - No bridging between adjacent balls
    - Ball voids: <25% of ball area (Class 2); <10% (Class 3)
    - Ball diameter uniformity: Within ±15% across package

3. COMPONENT PLACEMENT PASS CRITERIA
    - All BOM-required components present at correct locations
    - DNP components absent from board
    - No component reversal (polarity correct for all polarized components)
    - No wrong-value components (where value is visually verifiable)
    - Component body intact — no cracks, chips, or missing sections
    - Marking legible (where applicable and required by class)

4. PCB SUBSTRATE PASS CRITERIA
    - No delamination, blistering, or measling (subsurface damage)
    - No surface cracks in base laminate
    - Solder mask intact (minor scratches away from conductors acceptable for Class 2)
    - No copper exposure where solder mask should be present
    - Board warpage: ≤0.75% of board diagonal (IPC-A-610 target)
    - Via fill: Per design specification (not required if design does not specify)

5. CLEANLINESS PASS CRITERIA
    - No visible flux residue on Class 3 boards (must be cleaned)
    - Ionic contamination: <1.56 µg/cm² NaCl equivalent (IPC-6012 requirement)
    - No white residue, corrosion, or dendritic growth
    - No foreign material (solder balls, tin whiskers, debris)

6. AUTOMATED INSPECTION PASS THRESHOLDS (AOI System)
    - False call rate: <0.5% per board inspection (well-tuned program)
    - Minimum detectable bridge: 0.05mm gap violation
    - Minimum detectable missing component: Triggered for all 0402 and larger absent components
    - Board passes AOI when zero unresolved defect flags remain after operator verification
    - Note: AOI PASS does not override manual inspection findings

7. CONFIDENCE THRESHOLDS FOR AI/ML INSPECTION SYSTEMS
    - Defect classification confidence ≥ 0.85: Treat as confirmed defect, route to rework queue
    - Defect classification confidence 0.70–0.84: Route to human verification station
    - Defect classification confidence < 0.70: Log as uncertain, human inspection mandatory
    - For critical defect categories (bridge, missing safety component): Threshold ≥ 0.80 triggers immediate stop

8. FINAL PASS CHECKLIST
    □ AOI inspection passed (zero unresolved flags)
    □ ICT passed (if applicable to board type)
    □ Functional test passed (if required by specification)
    □ Visual inspection by IPC-certified inspector completed
    □ All rework actions documented and re-inspected
    □ Board traceability label applied (serial number, date, operator ID)
    □ DHR (Device History Record) complete and signed

REFERENCE STANDARDS:
- IPC-A-610 Rev G: Acceptability of Electronic Assemblies (primary)
- IPC J-STD-001: Soldering Requirements
- IPC-6012: Qualification and Performance Specification for Rigid PCBs
- Customer-specific acceptance criteria (where applicable — supersede IPC minimums)
