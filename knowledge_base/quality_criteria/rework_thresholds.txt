QUALITY CRITERIA DOCUMENT
QCD-002: PCB Assembly REWORK Thresholds
Revision: 3.0 | Effective Date: 2024-03-01 | Owner: Quality Engineering
Conformance Standard: IPC-A-610 Rev G / IPC-7711/7721

1. PURPOSE
This document defines conditions under which PCB assemblies shall be routed to rework rather than directly scrapped or passed. Rework is the controlled process of correcting a specific defect to bring the assembly to conforming status.

2. REWORK ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA
A defect is rework-eligible when ALL of the following conditions are true:
    a) The defect type has an approved rework procedure in SOP-RWK-001 or sub-procedures
    b) The rework attempt count at that location has not been exceeded (see limits below)
    c) The board's total rework event count does not exceed 3 distinct locations
    d) The rework can be performed without risk of secondary damage
    e) The defect is not on a Class 3 critical component that prohibits rework

3. DEFECT-SPECIFIC REWORK ROUTING THRESHOLDS

3.1 SOLDER BRIDGE — Route to Rework IF:
    - Bridge is accessible (not under component package)
    - Bridge is between signal pads (not VCC-GND direct short)
    - First or second rework attempt at location
    - No signs of thermal damage around bridge
    Action: Solder wick removal per SOP-RWK-002
    Post-rework: AOI re-inspection required; ICT required if bridge was between electrical nets

3.2 MISSING COMPONENT — Route to Rework IF:
    - Component is ≥0402 package (smaller may not be reliably hand-placed)
    - Replacement component available and verified (correct value, package, date code)
    - Pads are clean, tinned, and undamaged
    - First rework attempt (missing component = single rework event)
    Action: Component placement per SOP-RWK-003
    Post-rework: AOI + visual inspection; functional test if component is in active circuit

3.3 TOMBSTONING — Route to Rework IF:
    - Component body is intact (not cracked during tombstoning)
    - Lifted pad is not damaged or lifted from substrate
    - First rework attempt at that component location
    Action: Hot air reflow and repositioning per SOP-RWK-004
    Post-rework: AOI re-inspection; measure solder fillets at both ends

3.4 COLD SOLDER JOINT — Route to Rework IF:
    - Joint is accessible with iron or hot air
    - Component is not heat-sensitive (check datasheet Tmax)
    - First or second rework attempt at joint
    - No evidence of pad lifting from previous rework
    Action: Flux and reflow per SOP-RWK-005
    Post-rework: AOI; visual inspection at 10x; electrical continuity test

3.5 SURFACE CRACK — Route to Rework IF:
    - Crack confined to solder mask only (no copper exposure)
    - Crack length <2mm
    - Crack not in high-stress zone (mounting holes, connectors)
    - Board class is 1 or 2 (Class 3 cracks require MRB regardless)
    Action: Conformal coating application to seal crack; document with photograph
    Note: Substrate cracks are NOT rework-eligible — route to MRB/scrap

4. REWORK ESCALATION RULES
    - Rework fails (e.g., pad lifts, component damaged) → Scrap or MRB
    - Same defect recurs after rework → Escalate to process engineering, potential scrap
    - Rework attempt count exceeded → Route to MRB for engineering disposition
    - Rework creates secondary defect → Treat secondary defect as new rework event; count applies

5. CONFIDENCE-BASED ROUTING FOR AI INSPECTION SYSTEMS
    For AI/ML-detected defects, routing recommendations:
    - AI confidence 0.90–1.00 + defect type has SOP → Auto-route to rework queue
    - AI confidence 0.75–0.89 → Route to human verification first; human confirms rework routing
    - AI confidence 0.60–0.74 → Human inspection mandatory; human decides routing
    - AI confidence < 0.60 → Flag as uncertain; do not route automatically; manual review

6. REWORK TRACKING AND METRICS
    - All rework events tracked in QMS by board serial number
    - Rework yield target: >85% (boards successfully corrected on first rework attempt)
    - Rework escape rate target: <0.1% (reworked boards failing downstream)
    - High rework volume (>5% of production) triggers process investigation

7. REWORK COMPLETION REQUIREMENTS
    - Completed rework boards must be re-inspected to same criteria as new boards
    - Rework documentation added to DHR before board can be released
    - Rework notation must be visible on board traveler/label
